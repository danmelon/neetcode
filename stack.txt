Stack - Last in, first out -- great for 'most recent' problems

What is it?
> A stack is a LIFO - the last element pushed is the first popped.
> In python, a regular list with .append() and .pop() is a perfectly efficient stack (O(1) both).

Core Idea:
> Stacks shine when we need to remember the most reccently seen thing and compare it to current thing.
> Classic uses:
    > bracket matching
    > undo history
    > evaluating expressions
    > 'monotonic stack' for next-greater-element


Further notes:

> imagine pushing on to a stack and popping off a stack.
> Peek is also an operation - just look at the top of the stack but don't do anything with it.
